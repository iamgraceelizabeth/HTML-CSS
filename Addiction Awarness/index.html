Addiction Awareness

So, what is addiction?

Addiction labels the compulsive need for a habit-forming substance or the need to continue a behavior. On this website, we will focus on substance addictions. Addiction is a form of mental illness. Most evident in addiction is dysregulated behavior; in other words, the inability to regulate consumption. A person coping with an addiction could face the following:

  • Unstability when away from the substance
  • Lack of self-control
    • Have an increased desire for the substance
  • Dismissal or no acceptance that their behavior may be causing problems
  • A lack of an emotional response

Why does this matter?

Addiction is a very real thing and it has big impacts on the people and community around us. It affects physical, mental, and emotional health.
